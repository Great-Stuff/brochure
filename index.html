What is it

The legislative branch is one of the three branches of the federal government in the United States. It is responsible for creating laws. The other two branches are the executive branch and the judicial branch. The legislative branch consists of Congress and the fifty state legislatures Congress is made up of two houses: the House of Representatives and the Senate. The House of Representatives has 435 members who are elected every two years. The number of representatives each state has is based on its population. The Senate has 100 members, two from each state, who are elected for six-year terms

House of Representatives

The United States House of Representatives is the lower chamber of the United States Congress. It is one of the two houses of the bicameral legislature of the United States, with the Senate being the upper chamber. The House is composed of representatives who sit in single-member congressional districts allocated to each state on the basis of population as measured by the United States Census, with each district having one representative, provided that each state is entitled to at least one

What is it

Article III of the United States Constitution establishes the Judicial Branch, with members appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate. Congress determines the structure and organization of the federal courts, including the number of Supreme Court justices. Only the House of Representatives and the Senate can impeach and convict federal judges. Judges and Justices serve until they pass away, resign, or are found guilty by the Senate. The authority of the federal courts is defined by Congress, and they hear genuine disputes at the district, appellate, and Supreme Court levels.

The Supreme Court

The Supreme Court of the United States is the highest court mandated by the Constitution. Its Justices, determined by Congress, hold lifelong terms after nomination and Senate approval. While primarily handling appeals, its decisions are final. The Court focuses on interpreting laws and providing guidance rather than conducting trials. It plays a crucial role as the ultimate legal authority in federal law matters.

Judicial Process

The United States Constitution guarantees the right to a fair trial for those accused of wrongdoing, with additional protections provided by amendments. Criminal and civil cases follow a process involving arrest, charges, evidence review, trial, and potential appeals. Appellate courts review legal errors, while the U.S. Supreme Court may hear cases of significant legal principles or conflicting interpretations. The legal system ensures fairness, protection of rights, and resolution of disputes at various court levels.

arrow_back_ios Executive Branch

What is it

The President of the United States serves as both the head of state and the commander-in-chief of the armed forces, with control over the Executive Branch. They appoint leaders for government agencies, including the Cabinet, to carry out and enforce laws passed by Congress. The Vice President is part of the Executive Branch and is ready to assume the presidency if needed. The Cabinet and various federal agencies are responsible for implementing and enforcing federal legislation, each with their own distinct objectives and areas of focus, such as defense, environmental protection, social security, and securities regulation.

The (Vice) President

The President has key roles in the government and state, including law enforcement, agency leadership, and diplomacy. They update Congress, recommend actions, and are elected through the Electoral College. Joseph R. Biden is the current President. The Vice President's main role is to be ready for the presidency and oversee the Senate. Kamala D. Harris is the 49th Vice President and the first woman of color in this position. Their specific duties are decided by the President, and they have an office, residence, and designated transportation.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet consists of heads of 15 executive departments appointed by the President, involved in the Presidential line of succession and providing guidance. The departments focus on agriculture, commerce, defense, education, energy, health and human services, homeland security, housing and urban development, interior, justice, labor, state, transportation, treasury, and veterans affairs.
